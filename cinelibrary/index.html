What's new in v3.4 — Fill the gaps, on demand ✨

CineLibrary stays a fast, offline reader — but now, when you ask it to, it can reach out to TMDb to fill in a movie's missing details and even remember films you watched but never kept on disk. Always on demand, never in the background.

✨ Smarter, fuller details

  • Add watched movie — in Watched & Gone, record a film you watched but never had in your library. Search TMDb, pick the match, and it's saved as a record with poster, top-billed cast, plot and details — plus your note, tags and the date you watched it.
  • Fetch missing info from TMDB — a button on every movie's detail window fills only the blanks (poster, fanart, plot, cast, year, …). Matches by the movie's TMDb id, or a quick pick-from-list. Fill-only — your existing MediaElch data is never overwritten.
  • Sync to drive (state + fetched art) — write your personal state and any fetched poster, fanart and cast photos back into the movie folders, with the rest recorded in a tiny sidecar. It never rewrites your .nfo, so the drive stays the source of truth and a rescan can't lose your fill-ins.
  • Background material (Mica): Off / Subtle / Strong — pick how much wallpaper tint shows behind the sidebar. The poster grid always stays solid for smooth scrolling.

🎞️ Watched & Gone — the memory keeper it builds on

  • Keep the memory, drop the file — right-click a movie (or multi-select several) → Send to Watched & Gone. It leaves your library but stays a record: poster, details, your notes, watched state, tags and history all kept. Your files on the drive are never touched.
  • A page of its own — browse your records as a poster wall, search and sort them (Recently gone, Title, Year, Rating), restore any back to the library, delete a record for good, or export the list to CSV.
  • Completely isolated — records never clutter All Movies, Search, Browse, Collections, Statistics, lists, tags or Discovery. Statistics shows a quiet tally instead.
  • Review missing movies — when a scan finds files gone from disk, a tidy Review dialog pre-ticks the watched/noted ones to keep as records and lets you drop the rest — your choice, per movie.
  • Smart about returns — re-download or move a record and it automatically revives into your library at its new home, notes and history intact, no duplicates (matched by IMDb/TMDb id, then title + year). Left in place, it stays a record.
  • Safe & portable — records survive scans, cleanup, and removing a folder or whole drive; they travel with your portable folder and a Backup remembers them.

Why CineLibrary

🚀 Native, fast

WinUI 3 + C#. Cold start under a second. Scrolling through 1,000+ posters stays smooth thanks to an LRU image cache.

💾 Multi-drive aware

Index a USB drive, unplug it — its movies fade to "offline" instead of vanishing. Plug it back in and they wake up via WM_DEVICECHANGE.

📦 MediaElch-friendly

Reads .nfo + poster/fanart files alongside your videos. No re-scraping, no duplicate metadata.

🔌 Portable, offline

Install anywhere — external drive, USB stick, Documents. Library data lives next to the exe. No telemetry.

🎨 Light + dark

Theme-aware design, follows system theme by default. Mica backdrop on Windows 11.

🔄 Auto update notifier

Checks GitHub for new releases on startup. Shows a quiet toast — never installs silently, never nags.

Features

  • Library, Favorites, Watchlist, Statistics
  • Filter by collection, genre, decade, watched status
  • Search title, actor, director, plot
  • Grid + list views with S/M/L/XL density
  • Movie details: cast, crew, ratings, runtime, file path
  • Toggle watched / favorite / watchlist with one click
  • Export catalog as CSV or HTML
  • Keyboard shortcuts: Ctrl+F search, Ctrl+B sidebar, Ctrl+Q quit, Ctrl+Shift+/ help
